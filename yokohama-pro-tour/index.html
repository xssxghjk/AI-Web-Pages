Tournament Overview

About the Tournament

The Yokohama Pro Tour is a premier ATP 250 event held each autumn in the heart of Japan's most dynamic city.

🏆

ATP 250 Series

Prize money of US $670,000 with 250 ranking points awarded to the champion. Top seeds include world-top-20 players competing on outdoor hard courts.

📅

Tournament Dates

Main draw runs Monday–Sunday over 7 days. Qualifying rounds are held the Saturday & Sunday prior. Finals are played on the final Sunday afternoon.

🌤

October Weather

Expect mild autumn weather — highs around 20–23 °C (68–73 °F), low humidity, and mostly clear skies. Perfect conditions for outdoor tennis.

🎟

Tickets

  • Qualifying: Free entry
  • First rounds: ¥2,000–¥4,500
  • Quarterfinals: ¥5,000–¥8,000
  • Semifinals: ¥8,000–¥15,000
  • Final: ¥12,000–¥30,000
🇯🇵

Visa & Entry

Most nationalities receive a 90-day visa-free entry to Japan. Check your country's status at the Japanese embassy website before travel.

💴

Currency & Budget

Japan uses JPY (¥). The country is predominantly cash-based — carry ¥10,000–20,000 daily. ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post accept foreign cards.

Day-by-Day

Suggested Itinerary

A week that mixes world-class tennis with Yokohama's best sights, food, and culture.

Day 0 · Friday Oct 3

Arrival Day

Fly into Narita or Haneda. Take the Narita Express or Limousine Bus to Yokohama (45–90 min). Check in, explore the waterfront at Minato Mirai, dinner at the World Porters food hall.

Day 1 · Saturday Oct 4

Qualifying + Chinatown

Free qualifying matches at the venue in the morning — great chance to watch future stars up close. Afternoon: stroll Yokohama Chinatown (the largest in Asia). Try pork steamed buns (nikuman) for ¥400.

Day 2 · Sunday Oct 5

Qualifying Finals + Sankeien Garden

Morning qualifying finals. Afternoon off — visit the beautiful Sankeien Garden (¥700 entry). Evening: pre-tournament atmosphere at Kannai bar district.

Day 3–4 · Mon–Tue Oct 6–7

Main Draw Opens

Full day at the venue. Arrive early for practice session views. Night sessions under the lights are spectacular. Try the venue yakitori between matches.

Day 5 · Wednesday Oct 8

2nd Round + Yamashita Park

Morning match session, then afternoon break. Walk Yamashita Park and the red-brick warehouses (Akarenga). Sunset views over the harbor from Marine Tower.

Day 6 · Thursday Oct 9

2nd Round + Motomachi

Full tennis day. Evening: explore Motomachi Shopping Street and the hillside Yamate Western residences for colonial-era architecture.

Day 7 · Friday Oct 10

Quarterfinals

Two quarterfinal matches. Pick a side to root for. Early evening dinner at a local izakaya to celebrate your team.

Day 8 · Saturday Oct 11 ⭐

Semifinals — Best Day of the Week

The crown jewel of the trip. Two back-to-back semifinals. Electric atmosphere, fans in full voice. Stay for both matches and the sunset over the courts.

Day 9 · Sunday Oct 12

The Final

Trophy day. Arrive early for the warm-up. After the ceremony, head to Minato Mirai for a celebratory dinner with harbor views. Fireworks some years from the waterfront.

Day 10 · Monday Oct 13

Departure / Tokyo Day Trip

Late checkout. Option: quick Tokyo day trip (25 min by train). Shinjuku, Shibuya, or Asakusa all easily reachable. Fly home from Narita or Haneda.

Food & Drink

Where to Eat

Yokohama is a food city — birthplace of Japanese ramen, home to Asia's largest Chinatown, and packed with izakayas.

🍜

Ramen Museum (Shin-Yokohama)

Eight legendary ramen shops recreated in a 1950s Showa-era atmosphere. A must for any noodle fan. Try a "mini" set of three different bowls.

¥800–¥1,200 per bowl
🥟

Yokohama Chinatown

250+ restaurants in the largest Chinatown in Asia. Highlights: Heichinrou for dim sum, and the street-food stalls for buta nikuman (pork buns).

¥400–¥3,000
🐟

Yokohama Central Fish Market

Morning-fresh sushi and seafood bowls (kaisendon). Go before 8am for the best selection. Arrive by 7:30am to beat the lines on weekends.

¥1,200–¥2,800
🍺

Akarenga Warehouse Bars

The historic red-brick warehouses on the waterfront house craft beer bars, wine shops, and casual restaurants with harbor views — ideal for evening post-match drinks.

¥600–¥2,000
🍣

Umeyama Sushi (Kannai)

A beloved neighbourhood counter sushi spot near the venue. Chef Umeyama has been operating for 30 years. Book a week in advance for omakase.

¥4,000–¥12,000
🍖

Kannai Izakaya Row

The streets around Kannai Station fill with izakayas (Japanese gastropubs) from 6pm. Order yakitori, edamame, and cold Sapporo for the full evening experience.

¥1,500–¥3,500
☕

Bread, Espresso & Motomachi

Motomachi Shopping Street is dotted with excellent independent cafés. Perfect for a slow morning croissant before catching the day session.

¥400–¥900
🍦

Bashamichi Ice Cream Street

A famous strip of shops near the venue serving soft-serve ice cream in unique Japanese flavours. A local tradition since 1895.

¥350–¥600
Getting Around

Transport Guide

✈️ Arriving in Japan

  • Haneda (HND) — 35 min to Yokohama by Keikyu rail (¥560). Most convenient airport.
  • Narita (NRT) — 60–90 min via Narita Express to Yokohama Station (¥4,370).
  • Buy a Suica/Pasmo IC card at the airport — works on all trains, subways, and convenience stores.

🚇 Local Rail

  • Minatomirai Line — runs from Yokohama Station through Minato Mirai to Motomachi-Chukagai. Covers most tourist areas.
  • JR Negishi Line — connects Kannai, Sakuragicho, and Yokohama stations.
  • Single rides: ¥180–¥280. Day passes available for ¥460.

🚌 Bus & Taxi

  • Yokohama Loop Bus (¥200/ride, day pass ¥500) — hits all major attractions.
  • Taxis are metered and reliable; base fare ¥730. Useful after night sessions.
  • Uber operates in Yokohama. DiDi is also available.

🚴 Cycling

  • Yokohama has a popular shared bike scheme (Docomo Bike Share). ¥165 per 30 min.
  • Flat terrain around the waterfront makes cycling easy and enjoyable.
  • Bike lanes along Minato Mirai waterfront.

🚅 Day Trip to Tokyo

  • Yokohama ↔ Shibuya: 25 min by Tokyu Toyoko Line (¥280).
  • Yokohama ↔ Shinjuku: 35 min via Shonan-Shinjuku Line (¥680).
  • IC card valid on all routes — no separate ticket needed.

📶 Connectivity

  • Buy a pocket WiFi or SIM at the airport (IIJmio, Docomo Tourist SIM).
  • Most venues, cafés, and convenience stores offer free WiFi.
  • Google Maps works excellently for public transport navigation in Japan.

Insider Knowledge

Pro Tips

🎫

Ticket Strategy

Buy Semifinals tickets the moment they go on sale. They sell out in hours. For rounds 1–2, tickets are often available day-of at the gate — save money and stay flexible.

🌅

Arrive at Opening

The gates open 2 hours before the first match. Arriving early gives you access to player practice sessions on the show courts — completely free and often closer than match seats.

🤫

Japanese Tennis Culture

Japanese crowds are respectful and quiet between points. Follow the lead: no loud cheering mid-rally. Applause is generous and warm after every point.

📱

ATP Live App

Download the ATP Tour app for real-time scores, draw updates, and order-of-play changes. Essential for planning your day when sessions run long or short.

🥡

Convenience Stores

7-Eleven, FamilyMart, and Lawson serve excellent hot food 24/7 for ¥200–¥500. An onigiri (rice ball) + hot green tea from the konbini is the perfect pre-match breakfast.

♨️

Post-Match Onsen

After a long day on your feet, head to an onsen (hot spring bath). Most hotels in Yokohama have house onsen, or try Yokohama Manyo Club on the waterfront (¥3,000, open late).
