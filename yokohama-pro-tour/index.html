Between Events

Explore Shanghai & Tokyo ›

Make the most of your free days in two of Asia's greatest cities.

🇨🇳 Shanghai (Mar 28 – Apr 2)
🌃

The Bund

Iconic waterfront promenade facing the Lujiazui skyline. Best at night when the towers light up. 10 min walk from the tournament venue.

🏛

French Concession

Tree-lined streets, art deco architecture, independent cafés, and boutique restaurants. Great for leisurely exploration on a rest day.

🏯

Yu Garden & Old Town

Classical Chinese garden with pavilions and ponds. Surrounded by a bustling bazaar — pick up snacks and souvenirs. ~30 min from Pudong.

🍜

Xiaolongbao (Soup Dumplings)

Shanghai's signature dish. Go to Din Tai Fung or Jia Jia Tang Bao near People's Square. Cheap, incredible, and perfect pre-event fuel.

¥30–80 for a basket
🥢

Xintiandi & Tianzifang

Hip neighbourhoods with great food, bars, and street art. Xintiandi is more upscale; Tianzifang is artsy and maze-like. Both walkable from the Bund.

☕

Specialty Coffee Scene

Shanghai has one of Asia's best third-wave coffee scenes. Seesaw and M Stand are the local champions — find one near your hotel for morning prep sessions.

¥30–50
🇯🇵 Tokyo / Yokohama (Apr 6–8)
🃏

Akihabara — TCG Shopping

Card shops galore. Yellow Submarine, Hareruya, BigMagic — all stocked with FAB singles. A must-visit for last-minute deck upgrades before the Pro Tour.

Budget as needed!
🌆

Shinjuku & Shibuya

Shinjuku: neon nightlife, Golden Gai, kabukicho. Shibuya: the famous crossing, massive scramble, shopping. Both within 10 min of each other.

⛩

Meiji Shrine + Harajuku

A peaceful forest shrine in the middle of Tokyo. Right next to Harajuku's Takeshita Street. Good half-day trip before tournament days start.

🚢

Yokohama Harbour

Minato Mirai waterfront, the red-brick warehouses (Akarenga), and Yamashita Park. Great views, relaxed vibe, and excellent seafood restaurants.

🍜

Ramen Museum (Shin-Yokohama)

Eight legendary ramen shops in a retro 1950s setting. Try a "mini" flight of three different bowls. A pilgrimage for noodle fans.

¥900–1,200 per bowl
🥟

Yokohama Chinatown

The largest Chinatown in Asia — 250+ restaurants. Hit the street stalls for pork buns (nikuman) and explore the labyrinthine lanes.

¥400–2,500
Getting Around

Transport Guide ›

✈️ NRT → Yokohama

  • JR Narita Express (N'EX) — ~90 min, ¥4,370 to Yokohama Station. The most convenient direct option.
  • Buy an IC card (Suica/Pasmo) at the airport — works for all trains, metro, buses, and convenience stores.
  • Limousine Bus: ~90 min, cheaper but slower in traffic.

🚇 Tokyo ↔ Yokohama

  • Tokyu Toyoko Line: Shibuya ↔ Yokohama ~30 min (¥280).
  • Shonan-Shinjuku Line (JR): Shinjuku ↔ Yokohama ~40 min (¥680).
  • IC card valid on all routes — no separate ticket needed.

🚇 Shanghai Metro

  • Massive, efficient metro network. Line 2 connects Pudong (venue area) to central Shanghai.
  • Use the Shanghai Metro app or Alipay/WeChat Pay for ticketing.
  • Fares ¥3–9. Day passes available.

🚕 Ride Hailing

  • China: DiDi (like Uber) — works with a foreign card, but best to load WeChat Pay beforehand.
  • Japan: Uber works in Tokyo/Yokohama. Taxis are reliable with base fare ¥730.
  • Use after late-night sessions when trains are less frequent.

📶 Connectivity

  • China: Get a local SIM or portable WiFi at PVG. Google/WhatsApp are blocked — use a VPN. WeChat is essential.
  • Japan: IIJmio or Docomo Tourist SIM from the airport. Google Maps works perfectly for train navigation.

💴 Money

  • China (CNY): Alipay/WeChat Pay are dominant. Set up Alipay with a foreign card before leaving Germany. Cash still useful as backup.
  • Japan (JPY): Primarily cash-based. Carry ¥10,000–20,000 daily. 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards reliably.
Visa & Entry

Entry Requirements ›

German passport holders enter both countries visa-free.

🇨🇳

China

✓ Visa-free · German passport

Up to 15 days visa-free for German passport holders (updated 2024 policy). Your stay is Mar 28 – Apr 6 = 10 days — well within the limit.

🇯🇵

Japan

✓ Visa-free · German passport

Up to 90 days visa-free for German citizens. Your stay is Apr 6–13 = 7 days — no issues whatsoever.

🏥

Travel Insurance

Get a travel health insurance policy covering both China and Japan for the full trip duration (Mar 27 – Apr 13). ADAC, HanseMerkur, or AXA are solid German options.

⚠️ China note: VPN usage, payment apps, and internet restrictions are different. Install a reliable VPN (Mullvad, ExpressVPN) before you land in China, as the App Store/Google Play are inaccessible once there. Also set up Alipay before departure.
