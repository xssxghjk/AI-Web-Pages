Static Holds
  • Dosage. ACSM guidance calls for stretching major muscle groups at least 2–3 days a week, ideally most days, holding each static stretch roughly 10–30 seconds for 2–4 rounds — that's the baseline used for every hold/rep count above.
  • PNF vs. static. Contract-relax (PNF) stretching and plain static stretching produce comparable range-of-motion gains in most studies — so PNF is offered as an optional technique on split-focused stretches, not a requirement.
  • Learning a split. Consistency (3–5×/week) matters more than any single long session, and pairing passive static holds with active control drills (dynamic warm-ups, contract-relax) builds flexibility you can actually use under load, not just when relaxed.
  • Bouldering. Research comparing climbers finds elite climbers have measurably greater hip flexion and abduction than intermediates, and functional shoulder external rotation is linked to climbing performance — this module weights hip openers, rotational drills, and forearm/wrist care accordingly.
  • Flexibility vs. mobility. Flexibility is how far you can passively be stretched; mobility is how far you can actively move under control. The dynamic warm-up trains mobility, the static block trains flexibility — you need both.

Informational, not medical advice. If you have an injury or joint condition, check with a physical therapist before starting.
